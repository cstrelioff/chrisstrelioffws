about me



A physicist by training, I have substantial experience applying scientific reasoning, mathematical manipulation, programming chops and statistical thinking to problems in many areas of science. I am a long-time user of Linux and a dedicated Python programmer, but I enjoy the challenge of learning new things and have spent some time using many, many tools, ranging from HTML/css/js and LaTeX to Octave, R, C++, and Perl to MS Excel/Word/Access. In addition to my technical experience, I place great value in diversity, social justice, teaching & education, music, books and all things creative. Take a look at my projects and publications to get a sense of what I've done in the past and how I'm keeping myself busy at the moment.

I now live in Oakland, CA and would like to move into the tech industry as a data scientist, or a similar position. Are you looking for someone like me? Drop me a note I'd love to talk.

projects



sandbox

This is my current blog, where I discuss topics ranging from Ubuntu and Python to Bayesian and machine learning methods. Want to install R on Ubuntu 14.04? How about learning about pip and virtualenv for managing projects and package installation with Python? I have also started a series of posts about Bayesian methods, starting with the basics-- see here for more. These posts will eventually move to applications in Python and R. So, stay tuned.



livesoncoffee

This was my blog at wordpress between 2012 an 2014, covering topics related to using Ubuntu 12.04 for scientific computing. During this time the site has attracted almost 50,000 views (as of Sep 2014) and now averages 100 views per day. Not bad for a little blog about installing Python, R and vim on Ubuntu 12.04!
